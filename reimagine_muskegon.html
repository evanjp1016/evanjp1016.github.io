Reimagine Muskegon

Project Type: Urban Design, Social Media, Community Engagement

Date: 2014 - 2016

Role: Co Founder

Work Setting: Team of 2

Goal: Educate the general public of complex urban planning and design issues in Muskegon, Michigan

The Project

Overview

Reimagine Muskegon was a social media-based project promoting community, diversity, and city strength through urban design in Muskegon, MI. The general structure of the project involved, identifying topics of discussion - dealing with urban planning/design or urban spaces in Muskegon - and creating social media posts with an accompanying graphic to visualize the context and title of the post. These posts, primarily shared on Facebook, would include basic information on the topic, the history, and our points of discussion. In the comment section, community members would engage with one another other to discuss the post and share ideas and thoughts.

The name was a play on a 2003 community design workshop titled “Imagine Muskegon”. This 2003 workshop sought to take advantage of public input over three days and create a plan for a reimagined downtown Muskegon, MI.

This was the third social media-based project we created. Our earlier initiatives included Western Avenue Soccer Project, a proposal to create a small soccer field on one of the numerous vacant lots in downtown Muskegon, and Skee Town Ship Shop, a concept for a mobile pop-up-shop venue in Muskegon based out of a refurbished shipping container. Both of these earlier projects received interest by city affiliated organizations, but both projects faded as the officials and organizations lost interest when they realized we were no more experienced than college students.

We decided to remain anonymous for Reimagine Muskegon and let our work speak for itself.

The Product

Following

Throughout the course of the project, we reached thousands (9,745 on our most viewed post) of individuals and gained following and support of over 600 users between Facebook and Twitter, including numerous Muskegon city officials and outside organizations.

Reaching Goals

We succeeded with our goal to educate the general public of complex urban planning and design issues in a simple way that would foster engaged discussion without much guidance or mediation from us as administrators.
