Peck & Sanford

Summary

Peck & Sanford is a concept television sitcom based in Muskegon, MI. The series follows two bus drivers for the Muskegon Transportation Authority, and their regular riders.

Characters

Muskegon, Michigan: The largest city on the shoreline of West Michigan. A diverse, historic, rustbelt city trying to redevelop after industry left in the mid/late 1900’s.

Peck: The day-time driver of the #62 bus-line. A friendly, social man in his late 20’s/early 30’s with an interest in Muskegon history.

Sanford: The night-time driver of the #62 bus-line. A grumpy, quiet man in his late 40’s. His primary interest is his classic Chevrolet Impala.

Jane: an aspiring urban planner attending Muskegon Community College for a few years to save money before transfering to a larger university.

Filmore: A friendly, wise man in his mid 70’s. Retired from the old Brunswick Bowling headquarters in Muskegon, he is now an avid yardsaler.

Charlie: A young college graduate working in the Economic Development Department with the City of Muskegon.

Erina: An incredibly kind, always uplifting middle-aged woman working in the dining area of Muskegon Community College.

Lamont: An always-happy junior attending Muskegon High School. Erina’s only son.

Honest Ronnie: The owner of the delicious, but brutally honest and often snarky Honest Ronnie’s Delicatessen, the last cheap, good deli in Muskegon.

How it Came to Be

The idea behind Peck & Sanford sprouted out of an interesting encounter one day early in the winter of 2017. My brother’s girlfriend was visiting Muskegon for a few days, and she was taking a Greyhound Bus into the newly constructed Muskegon Bus Depot. On the way to pick her up from the depot, my father, brother, and I came across the Muskegon Area Transportation Services’ Peck & Sanford bus-line bus numerous times.

With the bus line already in our heads, we pulled into the bus depot in downtown Muskegon only to spot the Peck & Sanford bus-line bus drivers switching shifts. The driver entering the bus was a friendly-looking man, while the driver exiting the bus had an uncanny resemblance to Danny Trejo. We made a few jokes about creating a TV show following the lives of the two bus drivers. A few days later, the jokes and plot ideas were still running strong, and we decided to start writing.

The Brilliance of the Series

While at first glance, the series seems just like any other cliche TV sitcom, the brilliance (or at least brilliance in my eyes) comes from the first character listed above: Muskegon, Michigan. Sure, this is technically a setting, but we’ve constructed the show in a manner that Muskegon is just as much a character as it is a setting. The culture, people, weather, and history are just as crucial to the show as a normal character’s personality, perspectives, moods, and background.

Aside from this, each episode seamlessly weaves recurring themes, jokes, and details into the span of the larger plot of the show, allowing the audience to put one and one together and find subtle comedy in the subtext of the series. With an eclectic style, learning from shows like Seinfeld, Frasier, and The Office as well as directors and writers such as Buster Keaton, Aaron Sorkin, and David Fincher, Peck & Sanford aims to bring back timeless television comedy with the beautiful, modern look of many of today’s television series and films.
